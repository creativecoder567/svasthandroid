What is the yellow part of the milk?

When cow’s raw milk is kept in a container undisturbed for a while, the fat globules will rise to the surface. The fat globules have beta-carotene, coming from grass and plants that the cows feed on. Beta-carotene has a yellowish tinge. So the undisturbed milk surface will have the yellow ting.

How do you maintain the hygiene of the product?

Pure milk from farmers is procured directly and is bottled and sealed. Before sealing, the bottle is checked for its cleanliness. The steps are:

1.Fill hot water and add dishwashing liquid.
2.Rotate the bottle with bottle nipple brush inside until the bottle is clean.
3.One by one rinse all bottles under running water to wash away all traces of soapy water.
4.Place them in a bucket to dry.

Thus we ensure that the procured milk is reached to the end consumer in a healthier and hygienic way.

Can we give this milk to below one age babies?

In general, only breastmilk or formula should be used if your baby is less than six months old. Can we give this milk to lactose allergic person?
